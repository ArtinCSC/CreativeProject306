Defendant_Client Witness:   John Scott, CEO of MacRonalds

  • John Scott is the CEO and founder of MacRonalds. He’s had several years of management experience in the food and business industry prior to his entrepreneurship. He has strong connections within his network and continuously bests his competitors. He is cunning, intimidating, and tends to get what he wants. His subordinates tremble at his presence and his colleagues have nothing but respect for him. He has always been a bit of a geek when it comes to technology and relishes the idea of staying ahead of the curve.

    He’s been looking to integrate some of the latest and greatest technology into his chain of MacRonalds restaurants for a little while now. When he finally got the word from his lead software engineer that the automated drive-thru robots were now complete and ready for use, he immediately began rolling out this revolutionary technology to as many of his chains as he can. Now he will save hundreds and thousands of dollars on expending wages to drive-thru employees as well as playing a pivotal role in the advancement of Artificial Intelligence. Not only does this reaffirm his number one mentality as a CEO, he is on his way to becoming a world renowned public figure.

  • Secret Information: [Don't tell anyone this unless they specifically confront you]

    • While his robot was still in the early testing stages, one of his connections notified him that his business competitor is planning to release their version of the automated robot drive-thru. Since he has been working on this technology for a while, and he believes that MacRonalds is number one in innovation, he rushes the tests to be the first to the market. John Scott is made aware from his lead software engineer Jesse Smith that the robot is not yet ready for a public release and that it still needs a couple of more months to be bug-free. Being the person that he is, he successfully threatens to fire him if he doesn’t release the drive-thru robot soon, and Jesse Smith delivers shortly after.

  • Pro-Plaintiff Knowledge:

    • The released of the robot was rushed by CEO of MacRonalds.

    • The CEO threatened to fire Jesse Smith if the robot wasn’t released immediately.

    • The robot was not tested properly and thoroughly, due to the rush of release.

  • Pro-Defendant Knowledge:

    • There were indirect warnings available such as signs and brochures readily available for any potential user of the drive-thru.

    • The robot alerts the customer before handing out their order.
