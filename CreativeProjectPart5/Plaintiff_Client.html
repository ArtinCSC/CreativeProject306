Plaintiff_Client Witness:   Ethan Turner, Victimized Customer

  • Ethan Turner is a recent graduate at Eastview University of Virginia (EUV) with a Bachelor of Science degree in Management with a measly 2.7 GPA. He is a sluggish and lackadaisical individual who lives on a paycheck to paycheck basis. He is currently working as a waiter at a local Italian Bistro where his main source of income is from tips. Given his personality, he usually doesn’t make too much money on a daily basis. He dreams of eventually making use of his management degree as he watches the days pass him by.


    The day of the incident started in a routine manner for Ethan as he went to his shift at the Italian Bistro he works at. At least he had a party that his roommate Babbu had arranged to look forward to. After work, he was exhausted as usual and his mood had not elevated as the party progressed. Babbu noticed that he was considerably down and helped cheer him up. This led to Ethan deciding to make a coffee run to MacRonalds to help rejuvenate his energy level. Babbu insisted he buys additional coffees and food for everybody else, as he reluctantly agreed. On that note, he swiftly made his way to the MacRonalds drive-thru and was not aware of the newly installed, automated drive-thru robot. Although there were indirect warnings of the dangers that may be encountered, a direct warning was yet to be issued. He ordered 10 meals of a standard sandwich, fries, and a hot coffee which came to a total of $89.78.


    Ethan finally pulls up to the drive-thru robot and extends a hundred dollars worth of bills in one hand and 78 cents in the other hand to pay for his meal so he does not receive any coins back in change. Since he is a server, he tends to carry an assortment of cash and tries to stay organized in that respect. Realizing too late that the robot has failed to acknowledge the payment, the robot had begun delivering Ethan’s large order of coffees. With no hands available, Ethan cannot grab the coffee in a reasonable fashion and the coffee inevitably spills all over him. After suffering second-degree burns and agonizing over it for the next couple of days, he finds a lawyer to sue MacRonalds as he is appalled that MacRonalds would approve such a dangerous machine that failed to complete a standard transaction that occurs in the process of a drive-thru.

  • Secret Information:[Don't tell anyone this unless they specifically confront you]

    • Being the sluggish individual that he is, he finds himself lacking sleep on several occasions. In addition to that, his brother had recently passed away, which has caused him to lose even more sleep than usual. When it comes to the day in question, he only managed to sleep 4 hours. In addition, he had also just come off a 12-hour shift which could only exacerbate his grogginess further. He was clearly not in the right state of mind when going through the newly automated drive-thru.

  • Pro-Plaintiff Knowledge:

    • A direct warning was not notified to the user of the drive-thru, which may demonstrate poor care and safety to its customers.

    • The robot’s malfunction was the direct cause of Ethan’s burns.

    • A well-programmed robot should have realized that Ethan was paying for the meal and not well equipped to accept the meal.

    • Second-degree burns were suffered, thus visible and brutal injury is apparent.

  • Pro-Defendant Knowledge:

    • He slept only 4 hours and just came off a 12-hour shift from working as a server.

    • His grogginess may have caused him to neglect to read the signs, act in an outlier fashion, etc.

    • There were indirect warnings available such as signs and brochures readily available for any potential user of the drive-thru.
